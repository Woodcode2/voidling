# VOIDLING: Art Swap, Buildings + Houses (Rebuild Prompt 3)
Swap the flat sticker buildings and houses for the new clay-render sheets. This is the test of whether the clay style cuts clean through the existing pipeline. Prove the cutouts before shipping.

## GOAL
Replace the current downtown building sprites and suburban house sprites with cutouts extracted from two new sheets. Keep every placement, lot, and gameplay behavior exactly as it is. Only the images change.

## READ THIS FIRST (important context)
These two sheets are a NEW art style: soft clay-toy 3D render, NO thick white outlines, with a small soft contact shadow under each object. The existing cutout pipeline was built for the old flat sticker art that had hard white outlines. Soft edges and shadows are exactly where a flood-fill cutter can fail: it can leave a dark halo, eat into the object, or merge two close objects into one cutout. So do not assume the cutter works unchanged. Extract, then SHOW the cutouts, then judge them.

## DO NOT TOUCH (leave fully intact)
- Lot generation, lot density, and where structures are placed. Buildings and houses keep their current positions.
- All gameplay: eating, vacuum, growth, evolution, predation, scoring, spawning, audits.
- The Prompt 1 depth work (bottom-center anchor, foot-Y-sort) and the Prompt 2 juice layers. Reuse them, do not rewrite them.
- Camera, ground fills, roads, river, waterfall, and the void body rendering.

## STAGE 2: Extract and PROVE the cutouts (this is the real test)
- Run both sheets through the existing extraction pipeline on the solid dark violet background (hex 1E1338).
- The buildings sheet should yield about 10 separate cutouts: a glass skyscraper, a pastel skyscraper, an art-deco tower with an antenna, a mid-rise office block, a hospital, a school, a town hall with a clock, a corner cafe, a low apartment block, and a plaza fountain.
- The houses sheet should yield about 12 separate house cutouts of varying size and roof color.
- Report the exact cutout COUNT for each sheet.
- Then load the game and view the cutouts on the checkerboard using `?debug=sprites`. In plain words, judge them:
  - Are edges clean, or is there a dark shadow halo around objects?
  - Did any two objects merge into a single cutout?
  - Was any object chopped or missing a piece?
- If the cutouts are clean, continue. If they are dirty, STOP and report exactly which failure you see (halos, merges, or chops). You may then tune the cutter's transparency threshold and add a small edge trim to remove shadow fringe, and report precisely what you changed. Do not ship dirty cutouts.
