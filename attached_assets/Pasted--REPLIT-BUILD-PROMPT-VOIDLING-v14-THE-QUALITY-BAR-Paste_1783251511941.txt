## 2 — The Accretion Orbit (absorb redesign — this replaces match-3 gating)

Growth becomes simple and the black-hole fantasy becomes literal:

- **Every captured object enters orbit** at the void's rim, circling for **1.6–2.0 seconds** while spiraling inward, shrinking, and stretching slightly toward the center (gentle spaghettification — cute, not gross). At spiral's end it absorbs: THAT is when growth, score, the chomp animation, the pop, and the +N fire.
- Orbit capacity 8; if full, the oldest item absorbs instantly to make room. Living things flail while orbiting.
- At DEVOURER and above, T1–T2 objects use a fast 0.5s micro-orbit so rampage streams keep their popcorn feel.
- **Matches become automatic bonuses:** whenever 3 same-type objects are in orbit simultaneously, they auto-fuse — TRIPLE burst, bonus growth and score, banner — no player management required. Remove the old chip-ring UI entirely; the orbiting objects themselves are the visual now (they're prettier than icons ever were).
- Rival chip-theft (v12) becomes orbit-theft: their orbiting items transfer into your orbit.
- Tune total time-to-growth so overall pacing matches today (the orbit delay must not slow progression — front-load capture rate slightly to compensate). Evolution thresholds unchanged.

## 3 — Controls: minimum-latency pass

- Use `pointerrawupdate` where supported (fall back to `pointermove`), and process **all coalesced events** each frame so no input samples are dropped.
- Input is applied to the simulation in the same frame it arrives; rendering uses velocity-predicted position (one frame of prediction) so the void feels glued to intent.
- 2px deadband on the joystick vector to kill micro-jitter; all listeners registered non-passive where preventDefault is needed; verify no scroll/zoom regression.
- Feel target: side-by-side with any native game, direction changes must feel instant. If anything still feels floaty, reduce smoothing constants until it doesn't, and report the final values.
