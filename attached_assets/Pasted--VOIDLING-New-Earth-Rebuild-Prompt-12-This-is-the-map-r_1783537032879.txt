## STAGE 2: The fixed island (replaces the rotating plans)
Delete the rotating daily plan system and author ONE fixed district layout, matching the attached reference:
- A central PLAZA with the downtown core packed around it: towers wall to wall along the block faces, gaps between adjacent downtown buildings no more than a quarter of a building width, tallest towers nearest the plaza, civic buildings (hospital, school, town hall) in the core.
- TWO suburb districts: a cozy district using the small cottages, and a fancier district using the bigger homes. Tight residential blocks of roughly 4 lots by 2 rows on a small road grid. 120 or more house lots total.
- A PARK district beside downtown for the park props.
- A FOREST district toward the river side, containing a reserved ZOO clearing: paint its paths and enclosure outlines into the ground now, leave it unpopulated (inhabitants come next prompt).
- A BEACH band along one island edge for the beach props.
- A reserved AIRPORT strip: paint a runway with markings and an apron into the ground now, leave it unpopulated.
- A small reserved MILITARY pad, unpopulated for now.
- The RIVER enters from the forest side, curves past the park, and ends at the EXISTING waterfall exit coordinate.
Scale rule: keep MAP_SIZE and the void's scale unchanged. Tune road spacing and block pitch for density, so the island reads packed like the reference, not sparse.

## STAGE 3: Bake the ground (into the existing cached ground buffer)
Pattern-fill the surfaces: tex_grass everywhere green, tex_forest under the forest, tex_sand on the beach, tex_street on all roads, tex_sidewalk as sidewalks along BOTH sides of every road, tex_water in the river channel and lagoon (the existing scrolling highlights stay on top).
Then bake the detail over the textures: lane dashes and crosswalks, curbs, a fenced yard rectangle around every house lot (fence lines plus a slightly different lawn tint per yard), a driveway from each house to its street, flowerbeds, subtle mowing-stripe tint bands over the grass, plaza paving from the sidewalk tile at a larger scale, zoo paths and enclosure outlines, runway markings.
Texture scale: start around one tile per 2 to 3 house widths and adjust until the grain reads at gameplay zoom. Mirror-tile (flip alternate tiles) to hide any seams. All of this bakes once into the cached buffer: zero per-frame cost.

## STAGE 4: Re-target the world
- Structures use the new flat-bottom cutouts, anchored bottom-center on their lots, in the existing foot-Y-sorted pass. The new sheets carry NO shadows, so draw a soft procedural contact shadow under every structure. Retire the old yard-based cutouts from use.
- Scenery scatter re-runs against the new zones: forest fill in the forest, park props in the park, beach props on the sand, greenery across the island, same gating and caps as before.
- Food, people, and vehicle spawners read the new districts. The new road graph must produce working junctions so cars drive and turn exactly as before.
- Spawn breathing room: at match start, no more than 5 food items within about 3 void-widths of the player spawn. The feast belongs out in the world, not piled on the player.
