**Block types (assign across the 9):**

A lot = pastel house (varied body colors — butter, rose, sky, sage — white trim, triangle roof in a deeper tone, door, 2 windows), a driveway with a parked car, and yard objects scattered logically: garden gnome, 2–3 flower pots, mailbox at the curb, trash cans beside the house, a bike, occasionally a doghouse with a dog, a sandbox, a birdbath.

Eating it triggers a finale: slow-mo, full confetti, banner "YOU ATE THE WATER TOWER," +500 coins, and its own line on the share card.

Total population 250–350 objects. **Density rule: from any camera position mid-game, at least 12 edible objects are on screen.** If a region empties, trickle-respawn small T1–T2 objects at map edges (fade in, never inside the visible screen).

**A living world:** 6–10 cars drive the roads at 60 px/s, pausing at intersections; if the player (smaller than a car) is adjacent, the car honks (short synth honk) — if the player is bigger, the car swerves and flees at 140 px/s. People wander sidewalks at 30 px/s and flee at 120 px/s with a "!" and scrambling arms. Ducks paddle the pond. Dogs chase ducks occasionally. The world should feel busy before the player disturbs anything.

## 2 — Suction physics (the missing feel)

Replace pop-on-contact absorption with a **gravity well**:

- Any edible object whose center is within **1.35× player radius** is captured: it accelerates toward the player center (up to 600 px/s), scaling down and rotating slightly as it falls, and is absorbed when it crosses **0.75× radius**. The chomp sequence from v3 (mouth, crumbs, pitch ladder, pulse, floating score) fires at that moment.
- Captured objects that are living (person, duck, dog) flail while being pulled. Objects being pulled can pass over ground decor freely.
- If the player moves away before absorption completes, the object escapes: it skids to a stop where it was, slightly displaced. Escapes must be possible — that's what makes suction feel physical rather than scripted.
- Non-edible contact keeps the v3 slide + shake + pulse. No sticking, ever.

## 3 — Movement feel contract (hard numbers, not vibes)

- Acceleration 2400 px/s² toward the joystick vector; max speed 340 px/s at base size (evolution bonuses stack on top). On release, decelerate at 1800 px/s².
- Direction changes retarget instantly — this is an arcade blob, not a car.
- Camera: position lerp 0.10 with a 40px dead zone (no micro-jitter), zoom lerp 0.06, player rendered 64–140px tall as before.
- Brushing an edible you haven't captured nudges it a few pixels. Small mass in motion everywhere.
