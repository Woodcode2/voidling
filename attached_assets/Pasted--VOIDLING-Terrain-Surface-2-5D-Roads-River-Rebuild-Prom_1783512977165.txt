## STAGE 1: Roads become part of the clay world (baked into the cached ground)
- Recolor roads from the stark flat grey to a warmer, softer clay-asphalt tone that sits in the game's palette.
- Soften the lane markings: less harsh white, slightly muted, gently rounded dashes rather than hard rectangles.
- Add a subtle edge treatment so roads read as slightly recessed into the terrain, not a flat sticker on top: a soft shoulder or gentle shadow along the road edges, and a soft blend where a road meets the grass or sand instead of a hard line.
- Bake all of this into the cached ground buffer so it costs nothing per frame.

## STAGE 2: River becomes a simple 2.5D flowing band
- Simplify the river into a clean 2.5D water band in soft clay blues with soft edges, not a harsh flat blue shape.
- Add a couple of soft highlight bands or ripples that scroll slowly along the river's direction to fake the flow, the same cheap technique as the waterfall's scrolling bands. This is the only animated part, kept as a small live layer over the cached ground.
- Keep the river connecting cleanly into the existing waterfall at the rim.
